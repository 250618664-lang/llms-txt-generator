What Makes a Good Example?

All good llms.txt examples share these traits:

  • One clear summary: A single > sentence that explains what the site does, not marketing copy.
  • Accurate page titles: Titles match the actual page name, not a keyword-stuffed version.
  • Descriptive sub-lines: Each page link has a one-sentence description that adds context beyond the title.
  • Honest audience: Write who the site actually serves, not a broader audience for SEO purposes.
  • Consistent URLs: Every URL points to a real, accessible page with content matching the description.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use these examples directly on my website?

Yes — these are copy-ready templates. Replace the website name, URL, and page entries with your own details, then validate before uploading to your website root.

Which example should I use?

Pick the example that matches your site type. SaaS product sites use the SaaS template. Developer documentation uses the Docs template. Content sites use the Blog template. Business or agency sites use the Business template.

What makes a good llms.txt example?

Good examples have a clear one-sentence summary, accurate URLs that return 200 responses, descriptive page titles, and realistic audience descriptions. Avoid keyword stuffing or guarantee-style claims.
