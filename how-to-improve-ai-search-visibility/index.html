Day 1 — Audit Your Current State

Day 1
  • Run the AI Search Readiness Checker to understand your current score.
  • Check if your site has an llms.txt file (replace with your domain).
  • Check your robots.txt for AI crawler policies.
  • Check if your sitemap.xml exists.
  • Note all findings — these will guide your fixes.

Day 2 — Create or Update llms.txt

Day 2
  • Use the LLMs.txt Generator to create your llms.txt file.
  • Include: site name, description, key pages, audience, and any relevant policies.
  • Write in natural language — avoid SEO jargon and corporate tone.
  • Upload to your site root (example.com/llms.txt).

Day 3 — Set Your AI Crawler Policy

Day 3
  • Use the AI Robots.txt Generator to configure your AI crawler policy.
  • Allow: OAI-SearchBot, Claude-SearchBot, PerplexityBot (AI search crawlers).
  • Block or allow GPTBot and ClaudeBot based on your content policy.
  • Ensure Googlebot and Bingbot are NOT blocked.
  • Add your sitemap URL to robots.txt.

Day 4 — Submit Your Sitemap

Day 4
  • Verify your sitemap.xml exists and includes all important pages.
  • Submit sitemap.xml to Google Search Console → Sitemaps → Submit.
  • Submit to Bing Webmaster Tools → Sitemaps.
  • Check for any crawl errors in GSC after 48 hours.

Day 5 — Add or Expand Structured Data

Day 5
  • Use the FAQ Schema Generator to add FAQPage JSON-LD to your FAQ pages.
  • Add Organization schema to your homepage if not already present.
  • Add WebSite schema with searchAction to your homepage.
  • Use JSON-LD placed in the <head> section of each page.

Day 6 — Audit Content Accessibility

Day 6
  • Check that key content is accessible without login or paywall.
  • Verify important pages render content without requiring JavaScript.
  • Check that your Open Graph tags are set on key pages.
  • Verify canonical URLs are set correctly and point to the preferred version.

Day 7 — Verify and Monitor

Day 7
  • Re-run the AI Search Readiness Checker to see your improved score.
  • Submit any new or updated pages to GSC for indexing.
  • Set a monthly calendar reminder to review your AI search readiness.
  • Monitor GSC for crawl errors and Bing for AI crawler activity.

What Does Not Work for AI Search Visibility

Many tactics that work for traditional SEO do not help — and sometimes hurt — AI search visibility. Avoid these common mistakes:

  • Keyword stuffing — AI systems can identify and ignore content written for crawlers rather than readers.
  • AI-generated content at scale — Mass-produced AI content without expertise signals is low-value in AI search.
  • Blocking AI crawlers and expecting AI visibility — If AI crawlers cannot access your content, they cannot reference it.
  • Paywalling core content and expecting AI citations — AI systems can only reference content they can access.
  • Buying links for "authority" — AI systems are sophisticated enough to identify artificial link schemes.
⚠️ AI search visibility is not a ranking factor. It is a measure of how well AI systems can understand and potentially reference your content. Content quality and relevance remain the primary drivers of whether your site is cited in AI-generated responses.
